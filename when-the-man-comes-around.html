Overall Meaning

The song "When The Man Comes Around" by Johnny Cash is a haunting depiction of the end times. The song begins with a spoken introduction, describing a vision of the four horsemen of the apocalypse, with the first one riding on a white horse. The lyrics then focus on a man who is taking names and deciding who to free and who to blame. The song suggests that not everyone will be treated equally and there will be a hierarchy when the man comes around. The idea of a golden ladder represents the concept of reaching heaven or eternal life after death while the potter's ground is symbolic of being lost or forgotten.


Cash's lyrics are filled with biblical references such as Alpha and Omega's kingdom come and Armageddon. The suggestion is that the end times are near and that the final judgment is coming. There is a sense of impending doom and terror in the lyrics, as the trumpets and pipers play and multitudes march to the big kettle drum. The whirlwind in the thorn tree is symbolic of chaos and uncertainty, while the virgins trimming their wicks and the crowns being cast at the feet of the wise men suggest the end of life on earth.


The song is a powerful allegory that forces listeners to confront their own mortality and consider the consequences of their actions. It is a warning that the end is near and that judgment is coming. The chorus is a call to action, urging listeners to listen to the words long written down when the man comes around.
