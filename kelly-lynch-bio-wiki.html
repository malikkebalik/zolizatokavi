Kelly Lynch Bio (Wiki)

Who is Kelly Lynch?

Kelly Lynch is an American actress and model who is popular for her performances in ‘Drugstore Cowboy’ and ‘The Beans of Egypt, Maine’. She is also well recognized for her roles in the drama series ‘The L Word’, ‘Magic City, and ‘90210’.

How old is Kelly Lynch?

She was born on January 31, 1959, in Golden Valley, Minnesota, USA. She is currently 60 years old. Her mother’s name is Barbara Jean Dingmann( a modern dancer0 and her father’s name is Robert Lynch( a restauranteur). She has got a sister, Robin.

Kelly holds American citizenship but her ethnicity is unknown.

Kelly Lynch: Education, School/College University

Talking about Kelly’s education history, she graduated from the Guthrie Theater Drama School, at the world-renowned Guthrie Theater, Minneapolis, Minnesota.

Kelly Lynch: Professional Life and Career

She started her career as a flight attendant and she began working as a model for Elite and subsequently landed film and television roles.

She made her breakthrough in the film ‘Cocktail’ in 1988 and played opposite Patrick Swayze in the action film ‘Road House’ in 1989. After that, she appeared in several films like ‘Desperate Hours’ in 1990, ‘heaven’s Prisoners’ in 1996, ‘Three of Hearts’ in 1993, ‘Imaginary Crimes’ in 1994, ‘Mr. Magoo’ in 1997, and ‘Homegrown’ in 1998, and many more.

Kelly Lynch has portrayed the supporting role in the films ‘Charle’s Angel in 2000, ‘Joy Somebody’ in 2001, and ‘The Jacket’ in 2005. She joined the cast of The CW youngster soap 90210 from 2010 to 2011 as Laurel Cooper and portrayed the recurring role of Meg Bannock in ‘Magic City’ from 2012 to 2013.

Kelly Lynch: Awards, Nominations

She won Prism Award in the category Performance in a TV Movie or Miniseries for ‘Homeless to Harvard: The Liz Murray Story’ in 2004 and was nominated for the Independent Spirit Award in the category Best Supporting Female for ‘The Beans of Egypt, Maine’ in 1995 and nominated for the same award in the category Best Female Lead for ‘Drugstore Cowboy’ in 1990.
