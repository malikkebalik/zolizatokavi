    Xabi Alonso Facts

  • In December 1999, he made his first senior appearance with Real Sociedad in a Copa del Rey match against Logroñés.
  • He was 21 years old when he was first called up for the Spain national football team for a friendly against Ecuador on April 30, 2003.
  • He was part of the Spanish squad for the 2006 FIFA World Cup and on June 14, 2006, he scored Spain’s first goal of the tournament, his first international goal, against Ukraine.
  • On August 5, 2009, Xabi Alonso signed a £30 million deal with Real Madrid and he was given the number 22 jersey.
  • He signed a contract extension with Real Madrid on January 8, 2014.
  • He retired from international football on August 27, 2014.
  • Xabi Alonso moved to Bayern Munich on a two-year deal on August 29, 2014, for an undisclosed fee. The next day, he made his debut with the team starting in a 1–1 draw at Schalke 04.
  • On October 18, 2014, he scored his first goal for Bayern Munich which was a free kick in a 6–0 win over Werder Bremen. He then signed a new contract with the club on December 18, 2015, which would keep him at the club until 2017.
  • On June 1, 2019, he was appointed as manager of Segunda División B club Real Sociedad B.
  • Xabi Alonso went on to criticize British Prime Minister Gordon Brown’s economic policy in 2009. He was particularly critical of the devalued pound sterling and the 50% tax rate and warned that it has weakened English football and could drive top players away from the United Kingdom.
  • He has been a supporter of Meath Gaelic football.
  • He is a major Liverpool fan and declared himself the same even while playing at Real Madrid. In 2011, he was quoted in The Times Online as saying, “I am still a Liverpool fan and will be forever, absolutely” and said that he would raise his Liverpool-born son as a Red supporter.
