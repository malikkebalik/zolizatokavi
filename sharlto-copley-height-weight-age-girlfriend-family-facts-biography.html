Sharlto Copley is a South African actor, voice actor, producer, and director who has been a part of films like District 9, Maleficent, The Last Days of American Crime, The A-Team, Europa Report, Seal Team, The Snow Queen 2: The Snow King, Chappie, Hardcore Henry, Free Fire, God: Serengeti, God: City, Gringo, Beast, and Ted K. In addition to that, his television roles including playing Christian Walker in the action crime drama series Powers and Chez in the comedy-drama series Russian Doll.

    Sharlto Copley Facts

  • His brother Donovan is a singer. He is part of the Hot Water band, and his sister Marisa is a clothing designer and a dancer.
  • Before he became an actor, he was working as a television executive.
  • He loves creating visual effects for movies.
  • He has his own thriller which isn’t released.
  • He is a huge fan of the TV series A-Team (1983–1987).
  • When he got chosen to play the role of Murdock in the movie A-Team (2010), he immediately called his mother.
  • Kruger, the character that Sharlto played in the movie Elysium (2013), was inspired by special ops soldiers.
  • Copley won the award for Favorite Hero back in 2009. For the movie District 9, Copley was awarded Best Performance of 2009.
  • All of his dialogues in the movie District 9 (2009) were fake.
  • Alongside Angelina Jolie, Elle Fanning, Sam Riley, Imelda Staunton, Juno Temple, and Lesley Manville, he co-starred as Stefan in the 2014 dark fantasy adventure film Maleficent.
  • From 2015 to 2016, he brought to life the character of Christian Walker in the superhero police procedural drama series Powers. His co-stars for the project included Noah Taylor, Olesya Rulin, Eddie Izzard, and others.
  • He portrayed the character of Ted Kaczynski (an American domestic terrorist and former mathematics professor who was a mathematics prodigy) in the 2021 historical crime drama Ted K.
  • He provided vocals to the character of Switch (a mentally unstable gadgets expert cape fur seal) in the 2021 computer-animated action comedy film Seal Team.
  • In 2022, he started playing the recurring role of Chezare “Chez” Carrera (Lenora’s boyfriend in 1982) in the Netflix comedy-drama television series Russian Doll.
