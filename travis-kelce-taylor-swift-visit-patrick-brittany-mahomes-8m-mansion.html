Travis Kelce, Taylor Swift visit Patrick, Brittany Mahomes' $8M mansion

Explore More

Taylor Swift is proving that “It’s Really Nice to Have a Friend.”

The pop star and her beau Travis Kelce paid a visit to the Kansas City mansion of Patrick and Brittany Mahomes on Tuesday, according to a report from the Daily Mail.

Per the outlet, Swift was driven to the home along with some security detail while Kelce came to the mansion separately in his Rolls-Royce Ghost.

Swift and Kelce spent a few hours at the $8 million home before the two returned back to Kelce’s $6 million abode.

The visit to the home came one day after the Mahomes celebrated the first birthday of their son, Bronze, though it’s unclear if either Kelce or Swift were there in attendance for that gathering.

The trip to the house was the latest bonding experience for Swift and the Chiefs quarterback’s wife, who have quickly formed a bond the past few months.

During an Oct. 22 game between the Chiefs and Chargers, Swift and Mahomes broke out a secret handshake in a suite at Arrowhead Stadium as Kansas City put the ball into the end zone with a 46-yard touchdown to receiver Marquez Valdes-Scantling.

A few days later, Patrick Mahomes spoke on a local sports radio show about how he and Kelce need to come up with their own moves to outdo the other pair.

“I need to talk to Travis because me and Travis don’t even have a handshake yet,” Mahomes told Kansas City’s KCSP 610 AM in October.

“So I mean, they’re ahead of the game on us. So we’re gonna have to get on the whiteboard, and we’re gonna figure out a handshake so that we can try to one-up theirs.”

Swift and Brittany’s friendship continued to blossom and the duo began “texting each other even when it’s not game day,” TMZ reported at the end of October.

In November, the two even shared a champagne toast along with Lyndsay Bell, the wife of Chiefs tight end Blake Bell, and Paige Buechele, the wife of Bills quarterback Shane Buechele, who used to play for Kansas City, in photos shared to Brittany’s Instagram.

On the other side of things, the Chiefs bounced back this past Sunday with a 31-17 thrashing over the rival Raiders in an AFC West clash, as Mahomes threw from 298 yards — 91 of which ended up going into the hands of Kelce, who snagged six catches.

In the process, Kelce became the fastest NFL tight end to ever reach 11,000 receiving yards, and Swift liked a congratulatory Instagram post the Chiefs shared to commemorate the milestone.

The Chiefs next take on the Packers on “Sunday Night Football.”
