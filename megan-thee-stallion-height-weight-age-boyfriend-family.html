Megan Thee Stallion is an American rapper who became popular after a video of hers went viral on the internet. She is the first female rapper that is under the label, 300 Entertainment. She released her first full-length project Fever on May 17, 2019. Her mother was a rapper as well. Her first mixtape was released in 2016 called Rich Ratchet which went on to be a hit. In 2018, she released her EP based on her alter ego Tina Snow and was received very well by the critics. Megan has always been very vocal about her sexuality and has often expressed that in her music and lyrics. In 2018, she went on to release the song, Big Ole Freak that managed to be on the Billboard Hot 100 at number 99. Her mixtape Fever received great reviews and was selected as an NYT Critic’s Pick. In 2019, she released the opening album track Realer‘s music video, which was inspired by the blaxploitation film style and her freestyle in the cipher was praised. She is also known to be the originator of the viral catchphrase “hot girl summer” on social media and a song on the same name was released on August 9, 2019. Megan had applied for the trademark of the phrase in the past. She was also nominated for the 2019 BET Awards in the category of “Best Female Hip-Hop Artist”.
