What is Marion Ross’s Net Worth?

Marion Ross, the American actress, has a net worth of $10 million. She is best known for her portrayal of Marion Cunningham on the ABC sitcom “Happy Days.” In addition, Ross has appeared in recurring or guest-starring roles on various TV shows, including “The Drew Carey Show,” “That ’70s Show,” “Gilmore Girls,” “Nurse Jackie,” and “Grey’s Anatomy.”

Early Life and Education

Marion Ross was born in Watertown, Minnesota, in 1928 to Gordon and Ellen Ross. Her family moved frequently before eventually settling in Albert Lea, Minnesota. As a teenager, Ross changed the spelling of her name from Marian to Marion in anticipation of her future fame. She studied drama at the MacPhail Center for Music in Minneapolis during her junior year of high school and attended Point Loma High School in San Diego for her senior year. Ross went on to earn a bachelor’s degree from San Diego State University.

Marion Ross’ Career Highlights

Marion Ross began her acting career in 1953 with a debut in “Forever Female.” She went on to act in numerous films and television shows, including “Happy Days,” where she played the iconic character of Marion Cunningham from 1974 to 1984. She also appeared in “The Love Boat” and “Brooklyn Bridge,” which won a Golden Globe Award and received a Primetime Emmy Award nomination during its first season. Ross received a 1997 Golden Globe Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress for her role in “The Evening Star.” She also worked in voiceover roles in several popular shows like “King of the Hill” and “SpongeBob SquarePants.” In 2018, she announced her official retirement from acting.

Personal Life and Lawsuit

Marion Ross married actor Freeman Meskimen in 1951 and had two children together. They divorced in 1969. She later married Paul Michael in 1988 and remained together until his death in 2011.

In 2011, Ross and four of her former “Happy Days” co-stars sued CBS for breach-of-contract, alleging they were not paid merchandising revenue owed under their contracts. A judge rejected some of their claims but the parties ultimately settled, with CBS agreeing to pay each party $65,000.

Marion Ross’ Property Holdings

In 1980, Marion purchased a Woodland Hills, California property for $285,000. Adjusted for inflation, that’s equivalent to $700,000 today. She spent $600,000 over the course of a decade building what is now known as Happy Days Farm. Additionally, she owns a home in Cardiff by the Sea, located near San Diego.
